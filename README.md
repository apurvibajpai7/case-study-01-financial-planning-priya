# 📊 Financial Planning for Priya

### Case Study 01 | FP&A Portfolio Project

> A financial planning case study demonstrating budgeting, financial analysis, forecasting, goal tracking, scenario analysis, and executive dashboard development using Microsoft Excel.

---

## Project Overview

This case study demonstrates how Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A) techniques can be applied to personal financial planning. A structured financial model was developed to evaluate monthly income, expenses, savings behavior, financial goals, and long-term wealth creation.

The project follows a real-world FP&A workflow—from understanding the business problem to performing financial analysis, forecasting future savings, and presenting key insights through an executive dashboard.

---

## Business Problem

Priya Sharma, a 25-year-old marketing executive based in Delhi, earns a stable monthly income but struggles to balance routine expenses while saving for multiple financial goals. The objective of this project was to design a structured financial planning model that supports better financial decision-making and long-term financial stability.

---

## Project Objectives

- Develop a realistic monthly budgeting model.
- Analyze monthly income, expenses, and savings.
- Track progress toward multiple financial goals.
- Build an emergency fund planning model.
- Forecast five-year savings growth.
- Perform scenario analysis using different savings rates.
- Design an executive dashboard for financial reporting.
- Provide data-driven strategic recommendations.

## Key Findings

- Annual Income: **₹6,64,000**
- Annual Expenses: **₹5,29,600**
- Annual Savings: **₹1,34,400**
- Savings Rate: **20%**
- Emergency Fund: **80% Complete**
- Largest Expense Category: **Rent (34%)**
- Estimated Five-Year Savings: **₹7.88 Lakh**
- Moderate savings strategy (30%) offers the best balance between lifestyle and long-term wealth creation.

---

## Strategic Recommendations

- Complete the Emergency Fund before allocating additional savings toward lower-priority goals.
- Increase the savings rate from **20% to 30%** to accelerate long-term financial growth.
- Reduce discretionary spending on shopping and entertainment where practical.
- Increase contributions toward the MBA Fund after the Emergency Fund is fully funded.
- Continue monitoring monthly expenses to improve cash flow management.
